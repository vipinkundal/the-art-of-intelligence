Phase 12

12. Generative Modelling

Generative modelling studies how models learn probability distributions well enough to sample new text, images, audio, latents, or structured data.

12.1 Autoregressive models

Understand:

p(x) = prod_t p(x_t | x_<t)

Learn:

  • Autoregressive language models
  • PixelRNN and PixelCNN
  • WaveNet
  • Teacher forcing
  • Exposure bias
  • Causal masking
  • Sequence likelihood

12.2 Autoencoders and variational autoencoders

Learn:

  • Autoencoders
  • Denoising autoencoders
  • Sparse autoencoders
  • Contractive autoencoders
  • Variational autoencoders
  • beta-VAE
  • Vector-quantized VAE
  • Latent-variable models

VAE evidence lower bound:

log p_theta(x) >= E_q_phi(z|x)[log p_theta(x | z)] - D_KL(q_phi(z | x) || p(z))

Reparameterization:

z = mu_phi(x) + sigma_phi(x) * epsilon, epsilon ~ N(0, I)

12.3 Generative adversarial networks

Study:

  • GAN
  • Conditional GAN
  • DCGAN
  • Wasserstein GAN
  • Gradient penalty
  • StyleGAN
  • CycleGAN
  • Mode collapse
  • Training instability

Original GAN objective:

min_G max_D E_x~p_data[log D(x)] + E_z~p(z)[log(1 - D(G(z)))]

12.4 Normalizing flows

12.5 Diffusion and score-based models

These are essential in a 2026 roadmap.

Study:

  • Denoising diffusion probabilistic models
  • Noise schedules
  • Variance-preserving and variance-exploding diffusion
  • Score matching
  • Denoising score matching
  • Score-based SDEs
  • Classifier guidance
  • Classifier-free guidance
  • Latent diffusion
  • Diffusion transformers
  • Consistency models
  • Distillation and fast sampling
  • Diffusion language models

Forward noising:

q(x_t | x_0) = N(sqrt(alpha_bar_t) x_0, (1 - alpha_bar_t) I)

Common noise-prediction objective:

L = E_x0,epsilon,t[||epsilon - epsilon_theta(x_t, t)||_2^2]

12.6 Flow matching

Flow matching and rectified-flow methods are now important alternatives or complements to diffusion modelling.

Learn:

  • Probability paths
  • Conditional flow matching
  • Rectified flow
  • Neural ordinary differential equations
  • Optimal-transport paths
  • ODE solvers
  • Distillation to fewer steps

Flow ODE:

dx_t / dt = v_theta(x_t, t)

Flow-matching loss:

L = E_t,x_t[||v_theta(x_t, t) - u_t(x_t)||_2^2]

Learning target

You should be able to compare likelihood-based, adversarial, flow-based, diffusion, and flow-matching generators by their training objective, sampling procedure, strengths, and failure modes.
