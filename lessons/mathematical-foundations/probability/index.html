1. Mathematical Foundations

1.3 Probability

Probability is the language of uncertainty. It helps AI systems reason from incomplete data, model noise, estimate confidence, sample possibilities, and compare competing explanations.

The simple idea

Probability assigns numbers to uncertain events. In AI, those numbers help models express how likely an output is, how much evidence supports a prediction, and how uncertain a decision remains.

Essential formulas

Bayes' theorem P(A | B) = P(B | A)P(A) / P(B)
Expectation, discrete E[X] = sum_x x P(X = x)
Expectation, continuous E[X] = integral x p(x) dx
Variance Var(X) = E[(X - E[X])^2]
Covariance Cov(X, Y) = E[(X - E[X])(Y - E[Y])]

Why it matters for AI

Many AI methods are probabilistic: classifiers estimate class probabilities, language models predict token distributions, Bayesian methods update beliefs, and sampling methods approximate quantities that are hard to compute exactly.

Probability also supports evaluation and reliability by helping you reason about uncertainty, variance, confidence, rare events, and expected outcomes.

Learning target

After this lesson, you should be able to explain conditional probability, Bayes' theorem, expectation, variance, covariance, common distributions, and why sampling is useful in machine learning.
