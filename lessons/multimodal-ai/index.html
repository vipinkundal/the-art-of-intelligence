17. Multimodal AI

Native multimodality is now a core frontier topic, covering combinations of text, image, video, audio, action, and sensor inputs.

Learn

  • Contrastive vision-language learning
  • Image-text alignment
  • Vision encoders
  • Projection layers
  • Cross-attention fusion
  • Early, intermediate and late fusion
  • Multimodal tokenization
  • Image and video instruction tuning
  • Visual question answering
  • Image captioning
  • Document understanding
  • Speech-language models
  • Audio-language models
  • Text-to-image generation
  • Text-to-video generation
  • Multimodal retrieval
  • Vision-language-action models
  • Multimodal agents

Important design questions

  • How are modalities tokenized?
  • Where are they fused?
  • Are encoders frozen or trained jointly?
  • How is temporal information represented?
  • How are grounding and hallucination evaluated?
  • Does the model connect words to identifiable regions, sounds, or actions?

Learning target

You should be able to compare alignment, fusion, tokenization, and grounding strategies across image-text, audio-language, video-language, and vision-language-action systems.
