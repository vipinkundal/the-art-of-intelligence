Phase 9

9. Reasoning Models And Inference-Time Computation

This is another major 2026 addition. Current curricula explicitly include reasoning models, test-time computation, and verifier-guided methods.

Reasoning-time loop

Generate paths Use prompting, decomposition, or search to produce candidate reasoning traces.
Check steps Apply verifiers, tools, tests, or voting to find stronger answers.
Spend compute Trade more inference work for better reliability when the task deserves it.

Learn

  • Chain-of-thought prompting: Encourages intermediate reasoning text before the final answer.
  • Scratchpad computation: Gives the model working space for calculations, notes, or intermediate state.
  • Self-consistency: Samples multiple reasoning paths and chooses the answer that appears most consistently.
  • Least-to-most prompting: Solves simpler subproblems first, then combines them into the full solution.
  • Decomposition: Breaks a hard task into smaller questions that can be solved and checked independently.
  • Tree of Thoughts: Searches a branching tree of candidate reasoning steps.
  • Graph of Thoughts: Allows reasoning states to merge, revise, and connect more flexibly than a tree.
  • Beam search over reasoning traces: Keeps the best few partial reasoning paths at each step.
  • Best-of-N sampling: Generates many candidate answers and picks the strongest by scoring or verification.
  • Majority voting: Chooses the most common answer across independent attempts.
  • Process supervision: Trains or scores the intermediate reasoning steps, not just the final answer.
  • Outcome supervision: Rewards only the final result, even if the internal path is unknown or messy.
  • Step-level verification: Checks each intermediate claim or operation for correctness.
  • Program-aided reasoning: Uses generated code or symbolic programs to perform exact calculations.
  • Tool-assisted reasoning: Calls search, calculators, code, retrieval, or APIs to ground the answer.
  • Code execution: Runs code to test hypotheses, compute results, or verify examples.
  • Search over latent or textual reasoning: Explores multiple internal or visible paths before committing.
  • Test-time adaptation: Adjusts behavior during inference based on the current task or feedback.
  • Adaptive computation: Spends more reasoning steps on harder examples and fewer on easy ones.
  • Inference-time scaling: Improves answers by increasing compute used during generation, search, or verification.

Reference model

DeepSeek-R1 is an important reference for reinforcement-learning-driven reasoning behaviour and for studying the role of verification, reflection, and post-training.

Important distinction

  • Fluency is not reasoning.
  • Long output is not necessarily better reasoning.
  • Self-explanation is not proof of internal correctness.
  • Benchmark accuracy does not guarantee robustness outside the benchmark.
  • A verifier can be as important as the generator.

Learning target

You should be able to compare prompting, search, verification, tool-use, and test-time computation strategies, and explain why stronger reasoning claims require checks beyond fluent explanations.
