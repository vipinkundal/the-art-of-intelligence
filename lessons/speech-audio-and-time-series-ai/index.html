16. Speech, Audio And Time-Series AI

Speech, audio, and time-series AI combine signal processing, sequence modelling, forecasting, recognition, generation, and source separation.

Signal pipeline

Sample Convert continuous signals into discrete measurements over time.
Represent Use spectra, features, windows, or learned embeddings to expose patterns.
Model Forecast, classify, transcribe, separate, or generate time-dependent signals.

Study

  • Sampling theory: Explains how often to measure a continuous signal so important frequencies are preserved.
  • Fourier transform: Converts a signal from time view into frequency components.
  • Fast Fourier transform: Computes Fourier transforms efficiently for practical signal processing.
  • Short-time Fourier transform: Applies Fourier analysis over sliding windows to show how frequencies change over time.
  • Spectrograms: Visualize time, frequency, and intensity in one representation.
  • Mel scale: Warps frequencies to better match human pitch perception.
  • MFCCs: Compact speech features derived from mel-frequency spectral information.
  • Digital filtering: Removes, emphasizes, or reshapes parts of a signal by frequency or time behavior.
  • Autocorrelation: Measures how a signal relates to delayed versions of itself.
  • Time-series decomposition: Separates trend, seasonality, cycles, and residual noise.
  • AR, MA and ARIMA: Classical forecasting models based on past values, past errors, and differencing.
  • Exponential smoothing: Forecasts by weighting recent observations more strongly than older ones.
  • State-space forecasting: Tracks hidden system state and updates predictions as observations arrive.
  • Temporal CNNs: Use convolutions over time windows to detect local sequence patterns.
  • Recurrent forecasting: Updates a hidden state step by step through a time series.
  • Transformer forecasting: Uses attention to relate different time steps and covariates.

Speech algorithms and architectures

  • HMM-GMM speech recognition: A classical ASR approach that combines hidden state sequences with Gaussian acoustic models.
  • Connectionist temporal classification: Trains sequence models without exact frame-level alignments between audio and text.
  • RNN Transducer: Predicts output tokens while jointly modelling acoustic time steps and generated text history.
  • Attention-based encoder-decoder: Encodes audio and attends over it while generating transcription tokens.
  • Conformer: Combines convolution and transformer attention for speech sequence modelling.
  • Self-supervised speech representation: Learns acoustic features from unlabeled audio through prediction or masking tasks.
  • Neural vocoders: Convert acoustic features or intermediate representations into waveform audio.
  • WaveNet: An autoregressive neural audio model that generates waveforms sample by sample.
  • Diffusion and flow-based audio generation: Generate audio through denoising or invertible transformations.
  • Speaker recognition: Identifies or verifies who is speaking from voice characteristics.
  • Voice activity detection: Detects when speech is present versus silence or background noise.
  • Source separation: Splits mixed audio into components such as speakers, instruments, or noise.

Learning target

You should be able to explain how signals become spectral features, how classical forecasting differs from neural sequence forecasting, and how modern speech systems combine acoustic representations, alignment, decoding, and generation.
