16. Speech, Audio And Time-Series AI

Speech, audio, and time-series AI combine signal processing, sequence modelling, forecasting, recognition, generation, and source separation.

Study

  • Sampling theory
  • Fourier transform
  • Fast Fourier transform
  • Short-time Fourier transform
  • Spectrograms
  • Mel scale
  • MFCCs
  • Digital filtering
  • Autocorrelation
  • Time-series decomposition
  • AR, MA and ARIMA
  • Exponential smoothing
  • State-space forecasting
  • Temporal CNNs
  • Recurrent forecasting
  • Transformer forecasting

Speech algorithms and architectures

  • HMM-GMM speech recognition
  • Connectionist temporal classification
  • RNN Transducer
  • Attention-based encoder-decoder
  • Conformer
  • Self-supervised speech representation
  • Neural vocoders
  • WaveNet
  • Diffusion and flow-based audio generation
  • Speaker recognition
  • Voice activity detection
  • Source separation

Learning target

You should be able to explain how signals become spectral features, how classical forecasting differs from neural sequence forecasting, and how modern speech systems combine acoustic representations, alignment, decoding, and generation.
